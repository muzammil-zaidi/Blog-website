Welcome back to the blog, and today we’re going to discuss the topic of creating awesome content! Creating content for your blog might seem straightforward, right? If you’re just starting out and unsure where to begin, a quick Google search or a prompt in can offer plenty of tips on blog content creation. But in this post, I’d like to walk you through my own approach. Let’s jump in!

I’ve already written several helpful articles covering different aspects of creating blog posts, like crafting powerful headlines, choosing the best types of posts, organizing content layout, and more. I’ll include links to those posts at the end for easy reference.
Alright, I’ll assume you’re relatively new to blogging and content creation, so let’s start by answering this question –

What is considered ‘Great Content’ in blogging?


This is an important question, especially since the blogging landscape has evolved significantly over the years; creating content alone is no longer enough. I’ve emphasized this in many other posts. So, what defines a great blog post today?
As you likely know, content is the backbone of a successful blog—and of any website, really. It’s the essential lifeblood that keeps everything running.
Today, many experienced bloggers understand how to create strong content. Yet only a few manage to make their posts truly engaging, setting a new standard of quality that stands out from the crowd.
You might be wondering, with so many other ways to create and share content online, is blogging still relevant? Isn’t blogging on its way out?
The answer is no—blogging is far from dead, and creating quality blog content remains incredibly valuable.
With that in mind, now is the perfect time to revisit and refine your content strategy! But what do I truly mean by this?
Your content needs to be truly exceptional to make a real impact. While it’s not impossible, it does require dedication and effort—but it’s worth it. A high-quality piece of content can stand the test of time.

Step 1: Brainstorming
The first step in creating awesome content is to brainstorm ideas. This is where you come up with topics that you’re passionate about and that you think your audience will find interesting. You can use tools like mind maps or lists to help you organize your ideas. For me, brainstorming is all about letting my thoughts flow freely and seeing where they take me. I often find myself thinking about my own experiences and how they can be applied to others. For example, I might think about a time when I overcame a challenge or learned something new, and then I can turn that into a blog post. Brainstorming is a great way to get your creative juices flowing and come up with ideas that you might not have thought of otherwise.

    Here are some rules to help you brainstorm effectively:

  1. Free them from fear:
    2. It’s very difficult for people to share ideas if they’re concerned about possible negative consequences. A process and a setting that helps people get past fear are critical for brainstorming to be effective. One key principle in creating this setting is to prohibit any evaluation (even positive evaluation) during the idea generation.
  2. Use the power of the group:
    3. Build, combine, and create new ideas in the moment. Don’t just collect ideas that people have already had. The building and combining is where the magic happens. Occasionally break up into pairs or small groups. This will encourage even more sharing and combining of ideas.
  3. Get some outside stimulus:
    4. Asking the same group of people to sit in the same room and review the same information they’ve seen before is unlikely to result in exciting, new ideas. Talk to your customers, talk to other experts, explore how other industries are doing it. Have the meeting in the park or in a museum. Bring some toys into the room. There are countless ways to shake things up; try something new every time.
  4. Encourage the crazy:
    5. Everyone has heard someone say at the beginning of a brainstorming, “every idea is a good idea.” And then there’s a collective eye roll because no one believes it. While it’s not true that every idea is a practical idea, it is true that every idea can offer useful stimulus for additional ideas. Sometimes those ideas that are tossed out as jokes can be the spark that leads to a new direction and a winning idea. Allow, encourage, and use every idea, even if only for creative fodder.
  5. It’s a numbers game:
    6. The more “at bats” you have, the more likely you are to hit a home run. Drive for quantity of ideas. Ensure the session is long enough to generate lots. If you only spend 10 minutes on brainstorming, don’t expect great results.
  6. Laugh a lot:
    7. Humor stimulates creativity, so let it happen. One easy way to start off a session; have everyone introduce themselves by answering a fun or silly question. One example you could use in the fall: “What’s something you DON’T need more of for the holidays?” Some of the answers could even start sparking real ideas for the session!
  7. Homework is required:
    8. Both individual and group efforts are critical for success. Expect and insist on individual preparation in advance and follow-up afterward. Ensure everyone knows the goal in advance of the session, and ask them to do some homework before they arrive. When the session is over, create an action plan that allows ideas to continue to be shaped and added to as you move forward.
  8. It’s not for amateurs:
    9. Effective brainstorming requires knowledge and skill, both to participate, and especially to facilitate. It’s a completely different set of techniques and expertise than running other meetings, don’t assume you can do it well just because you can run a great meeting. If you don’t have a facilitator in your team who has the skill to train the group and run the session, hire an external one, or get some training to develop the skills internally.
  9. If it looks like a duck, but doesn’t act like a duck, it’s not a duck:
    10. If you can’t, or don’t intend to, follow the guidelines for successful brainstorming, then don’t call it brainstorming. For example, a meeting that just becomes a stage for one person to spout their ideas isn’t useful or engaging. And if brainstorming is not organized and structured appropriately, everyone in the room will feel how ineffective it is, and they’ll be sure to skip your next session. Either set up for success, or don’t bother.
  10. You’re not done until you decide:
    11. You’ve all been in this situation; it’s the end of a brainstorming session, you’ve created a long list of ideas, and someone volunteers to type up and distribute the list. And ... that’s the end. There’s no action, or at least not that anyone is aware of. It’s fairly demotivating to spend time and energy generating ideas and then feel they went nowhere. Plan time for, and require the group to do, some prioritizing of ideas during the session. Spend at least an equal amount of time on converging as you do on diverging. Yes, you read that right. If you generate ideas for an hour, also spend an hour on selecting, clarifying, and refining ideas at the back end. If you leave the meeting with a huge list of potential ideas, that’s not success. You want to leave the meeting with a short list of clear ideas, and a plan for action on each of them.

Step 2: Research
Research is an essential part of creating awesome content. It helps you to validate your ideas and make sure that they are relevant to your audience. You can use tools like Google Trends or social media to see what people are talking about and what they’re interested in. For me, research is all about digging deeper and finding out more about a topic. I like to read articles, watch videos, and talk to people to get a better understanding of what I’m writing about. Research helps me to create content that is accurate and informative.

Step 3: Create
This is the fun part! With your research and planning in place, it's time to start creating your content. This can be in the form of a blog post, video, podcast, or social media post. The key is to make sure that your content is engaging, informative, and relevant to your audience. I like to think of creating content as a form of storytelling. I try to use language that is clear and concise, and to make sure that my content is easy to understand. Creating content is all about bringing your ideas to life and making them accessible to your audience.

    Here are some tips for creating effective content:

  1. Use a conversational tone:
    2. conversational tone is key to creating content that resonates with your audience. It allows you to connect with them on a personal level and make your content feel more approachable and relatable.
  2. Use storytelling techniques:
    3. storytelling is a powerful way to engage your audience and make your content more memorable. It allows you to convey complex ideas in a way that is easy to understand and relatable.
  3. Use visuals:
    4. visuals are a great way to break up text and make your content more engaging. They can help to illustrate complex ideas and make your content more memorable.
  4. Use humor:
    5. humor is a great way to connect with your audience and make your content more relatable. It allows you to show your personality and make your content feel more approachable.
  5. Use examples:
    6. examples are a great way to illustrate complex ideas and make your content more relatable. They allow you to show your audience how to apply the concepts you are discussing.
